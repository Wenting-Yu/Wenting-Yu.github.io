Eats Shoots and Leaves

Every May, my mother and I renew a symbiotic relationship with our neighbors, whereby we attempt to help curb their rapidly overgrowing bamboo forest by picking newly sprung shoots and in return, we get to feast on the fresh shoots. Amid the hours of labor that go into picking, peeling, and preserving the shoots, I’ve developed a newfound appreciation for nature’s eternal precision. Bamboo shoots are testament to the Earth as the ultimate artist.

Between Fire and Ice

Iceland’s popular nickname is the “Land of Fire and Ice”. Indeed, some of Iceland’s most striking landscapes include fiery volcanos and crystal glaciers. But during my visit there, I found myself instead falling in love with the subtleties of Iceland’s in-between landscapes – those rolling hills and muted colors that stretch on and on, until perhaps, at one point, they too dissolve into either fire or ice.
